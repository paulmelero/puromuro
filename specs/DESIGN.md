# DESIGN SYSTEM: PUROMURO – THE ARTISANAL BRUTALIST

## 1. Creative North Star: "The Living Wall"

The design system for **Puro Muro** is built on the philosophy that **"The Wall is the Origin"**. The interface must bridge the gap between the raw, structural stability of a brick wall and the fluid, expressive movement of a professional muralist.

**Key Visual Tension:** \* **Stability:** Rigid geometric grids inspired by brickwork.

- **Fluidity:** Intentional "paint-stroke" overlaps and realistic droplet accents that break the grid.

---

## 2. Color Tokens: Raw Material & High Intensity

The palette is rooted in the relationship between raw material and artistic intervention.

### Core Palette

- **Primary Base (Deep Navy):** `#182c4b` (RGB: 24, 44, 75). Use for backgrounds and professional stability.
- **Structural Eje (Clay/Brick):** Use an earthy orange tone inspired by natural brick to define structural elements.
- **Vivid Intervention (Saturated Orange):** A high-intensity, luminous version of the brick color to be used as the primary action color.

### Accent Tokens (High Intensity)

Used sparingly for visual call-outs and "paint drips":

- **Luminous Orange:** High-intensity accent.
- **Bright Yellow:** High-intensity accent.
- **Clear Light Blue:** High-intensity accent.
- _Note: Always use these against a Deep Navy background to avoid a "childish" or "playful" look._

---

## 3. Typography: Structure & Plasticity

The typography system balances rapid communication with "artisanal" character.

- **Headlines & Large Formats:** **Montserrat** (All-Caps).
  - **Visual Rule:** Apply intentional deformation/warping to titles to give them "plasticity," mimicking signs hand-painted onto uneven surfaces.
- **Body Copy:** **Hazelnuts**.
  - **Usage:** For all informational blocks and extensive text.

---

## 4. Components: The Masonry System

### The "Brick" Gallery

- **Layout:** A "Masonry" grid where each "brick" (tile) represents a project.
- **Hover Interaction:** Bricks should transition from raw textures to high-definition mural imagery.
- **Watermarking:** For purely artistic mural images, use the logo or imagotype as a watermark with a **maximum 30% opacity**.

### Information Overlays

- **Rule:** When placing text over images (e.g., event info or signage), use a **Deep Navy (#182c4b)** gradient or grey background at the base to ensure legibility.

---

## 5. Visual "Must-Haves"

- **The Fluid Stroke:** The fluid, imperfect line from the brand logo must be used as a recurring UI element (e.g., as a custom scrollbar or a divider) to link the digital space to craftsmanship.
- **Paint Droplets:** Use realistic "wet paint" droplet assets sparingly near the edges of content blocks to break the digital "coldness".
- **Asymmetrical Bleeds:** Allow mural photography to "bleed" out of its container, mimicking how a physical mural interacts with the surrounding architecture.

---

## 6. Prohibited Actions (Brand Safety)

- **No Vertical Orientation:** Never implement the logo vertically.
- **No Unauthorized Gradients:** Do not use gradients outside of the corporate Deep Navy for text legibility.
- **No Digital Coldness:** Avoid perfect, sterile geometric shapes without adding the fluid "artisanal" line or paint-stroke imperfections.
